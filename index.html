About Durga Puja

Durga Puja is one of the most significant festivals in India, especially in West Bengal, celebrating the victory of Goddess Durga over the buffalo demon Mahishasura. This festival symbolizes the victory of good over evil and is celebrated with great enthusiasm, devotion, and cultural fervor.

The festival spans over ten days, with the last five days being the most important. Elaborate pandals (temporary structures) are erected to house beautifully crafted idols of Goddess Durga, and people visit these pandals to offer prayers and seek blessings.

Durga Puja is not just a religious festival but also a social and cultural celebration that brings people together, transcending barriers of caste, creed, and social status.
